# Lead Search Strategy — Brochure as a Service

How to find small South African businesses that need a website, industry by industry, using free tools you already have.

---

## The Golden Rule

You're looking for businesses that tick **all three boxes**:

1. **Active and trading** — they have customers, they post on socials, they answer the phone
2. **No website** — or a broken/outdated one (dead links, "under construction", template placeholder text)
3. **Reachable** — you can find a phone number, WhatsApp, email, or Facebook page to pitch them

If a business has all three, they're a warm lead. Everything below is about surfacing those businesses efficiently.

---

## Channel 1: Google Maps (Highest Quality Leads)

Google Maps is the single best tool for this. Businesses listed on Maps without a website URL are literally advertising that gap to the world.

### How to search

1. Open [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com)
2. Search: `[service] in [city/suburb]` — e.g. "plumber in Randburg" or "hair salon in Durban North"
3. Scroll through results and click into each listing
4. Look at the business info panel on the left

### What to look for

- **No website link** in the info panel — this is your #1 signal
- **Has a phone number** — means they're reachable
- **Has reviews** — means they're active and have real customers
- **Photos uploaded** — shows they care about their presence but just haven't gone further
- **"Temporarily closed" or outdated hours** — might still be trading, worth a WhatsApp

### Search queries by industry

**Trades & Home Services:**
- "plumber in [suburb]", "electrician [city]", "painter [area]"
- "cleaning service [city]", "pest control [suburb]", "locksmith [area]"
- "handyman [city]", "tiler [suburb]", "waterproofing [area]"
- "garage door repair [city]", "aircon installation [suburb]"

**Beauty & Wellness:**
- "hair salon [suburb]", "barber [city]", "nail salon [area]"
- "beauty salon [suburb]", "lash extensions [city]", "waxing [area]"
- "spa [city]", "massage [suburb]", "brow bar [area]"

**Food & Hospitality:**
- "restaurant [suburb]", "takeaway [city]", "catering [area]"
- "coffee shop [suburb]", "bakery [city]", "butchery [area]"
- "food truck [city]", "shisanyama [suburb]"

**Professional Services:**
- "tutor [suburb]", "driving school [city]", "photographer [area]"
- "accountant [suburb]", "event planner [city]", "DJ [area]"
- "dog groomer [city]", "vet [suburb]", "nursery school [area]"

### Pro tips

- **Work suburb by suburb**, not whole cities — more targeted, less overwhelming
- **Start with your own area** — you know the businesses, you can visit in person
- **Sort by "Most reviewed"** — busier businesses are more likely to pay
- **Check their Google listing link** — if it goes to a dead page or Facebook, that's your in
- **Screenshot the listing** — when you pitch, show them how they look on Maps without a site vs. with one

## Channel 2: Facebook Search

Many SA small businesses live entirely on Facebook. They have a page, they post daily, but no website. These are perfect leads because they already understand online presence — they just need to level up.

### How to search

1. Go to [Facebook](https://facebook.com) and use the search bar
2. Search: `[service] [city]` — e.g. "hair salon Pretoria"
3. Click the **Pages** tab to filter to business pages
4. Open each page and check their "About" section

### What to look for

- **No website listed** in the About section
- **Active page** — posting regularly, responding to comments
- **Good engagement** — likes, shares, comments on posts
- **Phone number or WhatsApp in their About or posts** — your way in
- **"Send Message" button** — you can pitch directly through Messenger

### Red flags (skip these)

- Page hasn't posted in 6+ months — probably closed or not serious
- No phone number, no email, no way to reach them
- Already has a professional website linked

### Pro tip: Facebook Groups

Join local community groups like "[Suburb] Buy & Sell", "[City] Recommendations", or "[Area] Community". People constantly ask "does anyone know a good plumber/salon/caterer?" — the businesses that get tagged are active, trusted, and usually don't have websites. Add them to your tracker.

## Channel 3: SA Business Directories

These directories list thousands of SA businesses, many of which have basic listings but no actual website.

### Directories to mine

| Directory | URL | Why it's useful |
|-----------|-----|-----------------|
| Yellow Pages SA | yellowpages.co.za | Huge database, many listings have phone only |
| Snupit | snupit.co.za | Service providers by area, shows who has/hasn't got sites |
| Yoco Merchant Directory | yoco.com/directory | Small businesses using Yoco for payments — trading, no site |
| Brabys | brabys.com | SA business directory, basic listings |
| SA Business Index | sabusinessindex.co.za | Categorised by industry and province |
| Gumtree Services | gumtree.co.za/s-services | Service providers advertising — usually no website |
| Locanto | locanto.co.za | Classified ads for services |

### How to use them

1. Search by category + location
2. Click into listings
3. If the listing has a phone number but no website URL — add to your tracker
4. Cross-reference on Google Maps to verify they're still active

## Channel 4: WhatsApp & Word of Mouth

This is more manual but converts the best because it comes with trust built in.

### Tactics

- **Ask friends and family**: "Know any small businesses that don't have a website?" — you'll be surprised how many come up
- **Local WhatsApp groups**: Community groups, buy-and-sell groups, neighbourhood groups. Businesses advertise in these constantly
- **Church / school / sports club networks**: Lots of small business owners in these communities
- **Your own service providers**: Your barber, your mechanic, the person who cleans your office — do they have a website?
- **Walk your suburb**: Physically walk around and note businesses with signage but no website on their signage or Google

### The warm intro approach

The highest-converting pitch is: "Hey [name], [mutual friend] mentioned you might be interested in getting a website for your business. I build simple, professional sites for small businesses — can I show you what yours could look like?"

## Channel 5: Instagram & TikTok

Many beauty, food, and creative businesses are Instagram/TikTok-heavy but website-light.

### How to search

1. Search location tags: e.g. search "Sandton" and check the Places/Locations tab
2. Search hashtags: #JoziBarber, #CapeTownNails, #DurbanFood, #PretoriaPlumber
3. Browse the "Suggested for you" accounts after following a few local business accounts

### What to look for

- **"Link in bio" goes to WhatsApp or Facebook** — not a website
- **DM for bookings** in their bio — they need a proper booking/contact page
- **Active posting** — they're invested in their brand but missing the website piece
- **Local hashtags and location tags** — confirms they're SA-based and active

## Channel 6: Gumtree & Classifieds

Service providers on Gumtree are actively looking for customers. They're trading, they're motivated, and they almost never have websites.

### How to search

1. Go to [gumtree.co.za](https://gumtree.co.za)
2. Browse: Services > [Category] > [City]
3. Click into ads — grab their phone number from the listing

### Best categories

- Home improvement, repairs & maintenance
- Beauty & health services
- Catering & events
- Tutoring & lessons
- Photography & videography
- Cleaning services

## Daily Prospecting Routine (30 minutes)

Here's a sustainable daily workflow that builds your pipeline without burning you out:

| Time | Activity | Target |
|------|----------|--------|
| 10 min | Google Maps — search 2 suburbs, 1 industry | 5-8 leads |
| 10 min | Facebook Pages — search 1 industry, 1 city | 3-5 leads |
| 5 min | Check 1 directory (rotate daily) | 2-3 leads |
| 5 min | Log everything in the tracker spreadsheet | — |

**Weekly target**: 30-40 new leads logged
**Monthly target**: 120-160 leads in tracker, 20-30 contacted, 3-5 closed

## Qualifying a Lead (Before You Reach Out)

Before adding someone to your outreach list, quickly verify:

1. **Are they still trading?** — Check Google Maps hours, last Facebook post, call the number
2. **Do they definitely not have a site?** — Google their business name. Sometimes they have one but it's not linked anywhere
3. **Can you actually reach them?** — Phone, WhatsApp, email, or Facebook Messenger. At least one must work
4. **Would a brochure site actually help them?** — If they're a one-person side hustle doing 2 jobs a month, probably not worth their R1,500. Target businesses that are actively busy and growing

## The Pitch Angle by Industry

When you reach out, tailor the message to what matters to THEM:

**Trades**: "When someone Googles 'plumber in [suburb]', you show up on Maps but there's no website to click. A simple site with your services, area, and a WhatsApp button means those people become paying customers instead of scrolling past."

**Beauty**: "Your Instagram looks amazing but when someone shares your page and their friend Googles you, there's nothing. A clean website with your services, prices, and a booking button makes you look established and trustworthy."

**Food**: "People are searching for [cuisine] in [area] right now. Your Google listing doesn't have a menu or a way to order. A simple site with your menu, hours, and a WhatsApp order button fixes that overnight."

**Professional**: "Your competitors all have websites. When a potential client is choosing between you and someone else, the one with the professional online presence wins. A site builds trust before you even meet them."
